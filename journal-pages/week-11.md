## Project Statement

I then finished off my project statement, I found it quite challenging as I found that I had a lot of information to condense into such a short 300 word limit. I knew I had to keep the idea idea of the greek myths as that was the foundation for my concept so I kept that at the start. I also wanted to lean more into the idea of overconsumption of resources so I referenced how the lack of leaves on the tree was a stylistic choice due to the overuse of the worlds natueral resources. Then finally I also wanted to invite the viewers to participate with the tree which I added into a section at the end to invite them in. 

Afterwards I was also thinking about how I wanted to showcase the project statement, I knew I didn't want to keep it simple with just a plain paper next to the project so I started brainstorming. Keeping on them with the greek myths, in the garden of Hesperides is where the titan Atlas holds up the sky so I thought it would be really creative if Atlas was holding up my paper like it was the sky; this way it also linked back into my sculpture thematically. I started by modeling something I could put a piece of paper into, I did this in fusion as I've had previous experience with the app. Afterwards I found a model online that was freeuse [Model Link](https://sketchfab.com/3d-models/atlas-holding-the-earth-32002a1de62b4b3face6dd55043b6cf6). I then went back into fusion to edit it slightly removing the ball and adjusting his arms slighlty so he can effectively hold up the paper holder. I then did a bit of testing by printing a few mini models just to test that it would actually work, and it seemed good so I printed out the main body.

Afterwards I then added some cotton that I had laying around just to make it look like he was holding up the clouds and to hide some of the imperfections. As well I used Figma to design a nice looking A4 paper to actually display the text as I knew I didn't want to do all this effort just for the paper to be plain.
